# Tree Appreciation
## Presence-First Design Principles & Requirements

---

## 1. Purpose

Tree Appreciation is not a social platform.  
It is a **presence practice scaffold** that helps people notice, return to, and care for trees — and through them cultivate shared meaning, continuity, and community.

The application exists to:

- invite embodied attention to living trees  
- strengthen relationships between people and place  
- support emergent rituals and shared practices  
- preserve continuity across time without encouraging consumption  
- deepen ecological awareness through lived experience  

**The primary experience happens with the tree, not on the screen.**

---

## 2. Core Ethos

### Presence before interaction
The application directs attention outward into the environment, not inward toward the device.

### Sufficiency over engagement
Success is measured by meaningful return, not time spent.

### Encounter over consumption
Memory and meaning are surfaced as moments, not browsable feeds.

### Commons over ownership
Trees are shared presences; no individual owns their narrative.

### Emergence over orchestration
Practices and rituals should arise organically.

### Care over control
Stewardship is collective and relational, not administrative.

### Continuity over accumulation
The goal is layered meaning across time, not content volume.

---

## 3. Design Principles

### 3.1 Presence-First Interaction

**The interface must never compete with the environment.**

**Requirements**
- Interaction duration is brief and purposeful.
- Default states encourage looking up and listening.
- Avoid continuous engagement loops.

**Avoid**
- feeds  
- infinite scroll  
- attention capture mechanics  

---

### 3.2 Threshold, Not Destination

The app functions as an entry point into an encounter.

**Requirements**
- Provide orientation, not exploration.
- Encourage stillness and sensory awareness.
- Minimize steps required to begin experiencing.

---

### 3.3 Memory as Encounter, Not Archive

Users should not browse a timeline of past entries.

**Requirements**
- Surface at most one memory or trace at a time.
- Surface memories based on contextual resonance (season, weather, time of day).
- Treat memories as offerings rather than inventory.

**Design intent:** memory arrives like a gift.

---

### 3.4 Embodied Ritual Support

The system reinforces physical practices.

**Examples**
- pausing and touching bark  
- seasonal return visits  
- listening before inscription  
- dawn movement practice  
- lantern gatherings  
- climate witnessing  

**Requirements**
- Provide gentle prompts encouraging physical presence.
- Avoid instruction-heavy guidance.
- Respect silence as participation.

---

### 3.5 Light Discovery & Legibility

The system helps people notice that a place is meaningful.

**Requirements**
- Provide subtle markers indicating shared presence.
- Surface rhythms (e.g., dawn movement, solstice lanterns).
- Avoid scheduled programming aesthetics.

---

### 3.6 Seasonal & Environmental Resonance

Memory and awareness align with ecological cycles.

**Requirements**
- Surface blossom notes in spring, shade notes in heat, storm notes in wind.
- Allow climate observations to accumulate over years.
- Integrate environmental data only when it deepens lived awareness.

---

### 3.7 Social Emergence & Relationship Formation

The application increases the likelihood of repeated co-presence.

**Requirements**
- Encourage return patterns.
- Normalize stillness in public space.
- Avoid identity performance features.

**Desired outcome:** familiarity before conversation.

---

### 3.8 Commons Stewardship & Care

Shared spaces require tending.

**Requirements**
- Enable community responses to harmful inscriptions.
- Allow soft stewardship (flagging, contextual replies, restoration).
- Avoid heavy-handed moderation aesthetics.

**Design intent:** care is visible, control is not.

---

### 3.9 Minimal Technological Presence

Technology acts as quiet scaffolding.

**Requirements**
- Interface remains simple and low friction.
- Avoid feature creep.
- Prioritize durability and longevity over novelty.

---

## 4. Presence Practices Supported

### Individual
- stillness  
- reflection  
- grief processing  
- apology & gratitude  
- mindful breathing  
- environmental witnessing  

### Relational
- serendipitous encounters  
- repeated co-presence  
- shared quiet  
- intergenerational continuity  

### Communal
- seasonal gatherings  
- dawn movement practice  
- lantern rituals  
- climate care actions  
- ephemeral chalk or ribbon markings  
- tending storm damage  

---

## 5. Memory & Inscription Philosophy

### Inscription is intentional, not casual.

**Guidelines**
- Encounter presence before inscription.
- Writing should feel like crossing a threshold.
- Listening precedes speaking.

### Memory Layers

The system supports:

- emotional traces  
- ecological observations  
- seasonal markers  
- responses across time  
- intergenerational continuity  

It does **not** support:

- performative posting  
- high-frequency updates  
- visibility optimization  

---

## 6. Environmental Awareness & Climate Witnessing

Trees translate planetary change into lived experience.

**Requirements**
- Allow logging of seasonal shifts.
- Support long-term ecological observation.
- Integrate sensors only when they deepen awareness.
- Present environmental data in experiential terms.

Example:

> Shade here is 6°C cooler today.

---

## 7. Intercultural & Embodied Practices

The application remains culturally open and non-prescriptive.

**Supported expressions may include:**
- movement practices (e.g., qi gong)  
- breathwork and grounding practices  
- quiet prayer or contemplation  
- ecological rituals and offerings  

**Requirement:** plurality is normalized; no tradition is centered.

---

## 8. Harm, Friction & Repair

Commons include conflict.

**Requirements**
- harmful content may be contextualized rather than instantly erased
- community response should be visible
- repair practices should be encouraged
- allow fading and natural weathering of harm over time

---

## 9. Success Indicators (Non-Extractive)

Success is qualitative rather than engagement-driven.

### Indicators
- repeat visits across seasons and years  
- emergent rituals  
- multi-generational continuity  
- evidence of care behaviors  
- climate observations accumulating  
- relationships forming through repeated presence  

---

## 10. Design Constraints

To preserve ethos:

- No infinite feeds  
- No engagement metrics  
- No popularity rankings  
- No algorithmic optimization loops  
- No performative identity mechanics  
- No push notifications designed to increase usage  

---

## 11. Guiding Design Questions

Before introducing any feature:

1. Does this deepen presence?
2. Does this strengthen continuity across time?
3. Does this support care and stewardship?
4. Does this encourage embodied experience?
5. Does this resist attention extraction?
6. Would the practice still function without this?

If the answer to #6 is no, reconsider.

---

## 12. Guiding Principle

**People come for shade.  
They return for quiet.  
They stay for continuity.**

Technology helps them notice what is already waiting.
